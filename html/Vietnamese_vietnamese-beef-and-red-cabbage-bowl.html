Patty
04/07/2018
I gave it a 4 rating after making my tweeks. First I chopped up all the cabbage, the bowl idea was lost on me. I used a jalapeño that I cut in half and seeded, I took it out before serving so I just got the right amount of heat with no surprises. After the meat, onion, pepper mixture was cooked, I tossed in the cabbage to cook it a bit, then added the lime juice mixture and cooked it all together. After everything was well mixed and the cabbage a little cooked, I removed the jalapeño and threw it away. Then topped it with the chopped mint, cilantro, and raw onion slices.

Kirsten Herman
06/17/2019
I like to double this and use a mix of ground turkey and ground buffalo which makes a ton of food. Like others, I use a jalapeno to substitute for the listed pepper. Keep most, or all, of the jalapeno seeds in the recipe for a kick. I chop all of the cabbage and onions but don't add the cabbage until the last 5 minutes of cooking. This is excellent with sticky Jasmine rice.

Heather
04/08/2019
Delicious! Instead of the bowl, I just thinly sliced about 3/4 of a small red cabbage and ended up adding it to the cooked beef mixture and letting it cook slightly for about 3-5 minutes. I used grapeseed oil for the dressing/sauce as well as coconut palm sugar, of which I used about 1 TBs after tasting, as I find it's less sweet than brown sugar. I also subbed parsley for cilantro as DH hates it and 1 deseeded, chopped jalapeño which I cooked with the onion. I am sure I'll make this one again so thanks for sharing a great recipe, Sahara B!
